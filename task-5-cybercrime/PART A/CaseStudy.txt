Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

Online payment fraud, also known as UPI fraud, is a cybercrime in which the victim is duped into sending money or divulging information related to their payment system. This form of cybercrime is on an increasing trend due to the growing popularity of mobile banking and online payment systems like UPI.

One form of online payment fraud is as follows: The cybercriminal contacts the victim over a phone call, SMS, WhatsApp, or social media platforms and pretends to be a bank employee or a customer service officer or a buyer/seller. Step 1: The cybercriminal creates a false sense of urgency around an issue that requires immediate attention, like an issue with an account blockage, a failed transaction, or winning an online contest and needing a refund. Step 2: The cybercriminal asks the victim to click on a link or share a UPI collect approval. Step 3: Instead of sending money to the cybercriminal, the victim unknowingly approves a payment request. Step 4: The cybercriminal then immediately withdraws money from the victim’s account and disappears.

College students, senior citizens, small business owners, and new users of online payment systems are more likely to become victims of online payment scams because they may not be aware of the different forms of online payment scams perpetrated by cybercriminals. People expecting refunds or those in online businesses also risk becoming victims of this form of cybercrime.

The consequences of online payment scams include financial loss, mental distress, and loss of faith in online payment systems. The victim may not be able to get their money back in case they report this form of cybercrime too late. The victim also risks becoming a victim of data theft, which may be used in other forms of cybercrime in the near future.
